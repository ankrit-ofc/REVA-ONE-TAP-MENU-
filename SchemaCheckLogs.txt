Restaurant Menu Structure

1. Category:
A group used to organize menu items.
Examples: Burgers, Pizza, Drinks, Desserts.

2. Product:
The actual item customers order.
Example: Chicken Burger, Coke, Margherita Pizza.

3. Variant:
Different versions of the same product that usually affect price, size, quantity, or preparation. Customers typically choose one variant.
Examples:
- Pizza: Small / Medium / Large
- Coffee: Regular / Large
- Burger: Single Patty / Double Patty

4. Add-on:
Optional extras that customers can add to a product for an additional charge. Customers may choose none, one, or multiple add-ons depending on the rules.
Examples:
- Extra Cheese
- Extra Patty
- French Fries
- Bacon

Hierarchy:
Category → Product → Variant → Add-ons

Example:
Category: Burgers
Product: Chicken Burger
Variant: Double Patty (+₹70)
Add-ons: Extra Cheese (+₹30), French Fries (+₹80)

Meaning:
Category organizes the menu, Product is what is sold, Variant determines which version of the product is ordered, and Add-ons are optional extras added to enhance the order.
